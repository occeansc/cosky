Section 1

Pre-Trade Preparation

COSKY operates on a weekly entry cadence with daily exit monitoring. The most common operational error is skipping the daily exit check — this causes sec_collapse exits to be missed, turning profitable trades into marginal ones.

1.1 Friday Weekly Preparation (Entry Scan Day)

#TaskDetail
1 SPY Regime Check Look up SPY close vs SPY 20-day EMA. If SPY is BELOW the 20-day EMA: versions 2.0, 3.0, 4.0, and 5.0 are closed to new entries. Only COSKY 1.0 may scan (no regime gate). Record regime status in signal log.
2 Identify current month Check whether the current month is strong, standard, or weak for each version being scanned. July–September are universally strong (3.0 and 4.0: 120% size). January is a danger month for 2.0/3.0/4.0 — skip or reduce to 50% size.
3 Count open positions Maximum 8 concurrent across all COSKY versions. No individual version should exceed 5 open positions. If at capacity, the scan output is watchlist only — no new entries until a position closes.
4 Run entry scans in priority order Priority: (1) COSKY 4.0 via Prompt C-1. (2) COSKY 3.0 via Prompt C-2. (3) Other versions as needed. Priority order reflects EV: 4.0 first, then 3.0/2.0, then 5.0, then 1.0.
5 Score and grade outputs For every qualifying signal from the AI scan, calculate the Entry Grade using the 5-dimension scoring system. Discard Grade 1 unless at full portfolio deployment. Record Grades 2–5 in the signal log (columns 1–13).
6 Sunday review Review shortlist. Check macro calendar for major events (FOMC, key earnings, sector-disrupting data releases). Adjust grades if needed. Confirm seasonal position size adjustments for the coming week.

1.2 Daily Exit Monitor — Every Market Day

🚨 The Daily Exit Monitor is Non-Negotiable

Run Prompt C-3 before or at market open every day. Sec_collapse (42% WR after trigger) requires same-day action. A trade that triggers sec_collapse on Tuesday but is only checked on Friday has potentially surrendered weeks of unrealised profit.

The daily check takes 5 minutes. Missing it is the single most costly operational shortcut in COSKY management.

The Dual-Track Grading System

COSKY uses two independent grade tracks simultaneously. Entry Grade — assessed once at signal identification — determines position size. Live Grade — dynamic during the trade — determines hold and exit behaviour. They serve different purposes and update on different timescales.

Critical finding from the data: all five versions have nearly identical EV per trade (+5.1% to +7.2%). The win rate hierarchy (4.0 > 3.0 > 5.0) is misleading. COSKY 1.0's 46% win rate still generates +6.7% EV because its average win (+21.8%) is the highest in the family. The version scoring reflects Kelly (consistency) as well as EV.

Section 4

Version-Specific Management Rules

Each COSKY version has unique characteristics that require version-specific operational adjustments beyond the universal grade framework.

1.0RS 52-Week High+134% · $234K · 119 trades
Highest average win (+21.8%) — when right, significantly right. The 46% win rate is not a problem. Expect individual losses and hold through them. The payoff structure requires holding to the exit signal, not exiting early on recovering trades.
No regime gate — 1.0 trades in all conditions. In bear markets (SPY below 20-EMA), 1.0 is the only COSKY version scanning.
January danger: the strategy's worst month for most versions, but 1.0 hits 50% WR in April (its best month). In November (12% WR for 1.0), consider suspending new 1.0 entries.
XLB has negative EV (−2.0%) for 1.0. Do not take 1.0 signals in XLB regardless of other conditions.
Exit: RSR below 38 → exit. RS at 52-week low → exit immediately. RS below 50-day EMA → exit. Stop (7% below entry) → exit.
2.0Regime + VCP + 2R+147% · $247K · 231 trades
Tightest average loss (−5.4%). The VCP filter (ATR(10) < 60% of ATR(30)) ensures price is in a base before entry. Entry has lower failure rate because price has already stopped declining.
SPY regime gate — zero entries when SPY below 20-day EMA. This constraint is a major source of 2.0's risk-adjusted outperformance.
November (26% WR) and January (0% WR) are danger months. Suspend 2.0 in these months entirely or reduce to 50% position size with Grade 4+ minimum.
September is 2.0's best month (82% WR, EV=+9%). May is also strong (64% WR).
The 2R partial (29 occurrences, 100% WR) and timeout exit (36 occurrences, 100% WR, avg +29.1%) account for most of 2.0's profitable outcomes. Letting these play out is critical.
3.0RS Reversal+93% · $193K · 150 trades · 64% WR
All six entry conditions must be true simultaneously. The RS must have been below 28th percentile 20 days ago, crossed its 20-day EMA, the EMA must be rising, and the rank must still be below 55th percentile. This precision produces 64% WR — the highest in the family.
XLI (EV=−2.0%) and XLV (EV=−0.7%) are negative-EV for 3.0. Never take 3.0 signals in these sectors regardless of grade score.
3.0 exits exactly where 1.0 enters (RS at 52-week high / crowd recognition). The strategy has been in the trade for 3–5 weeks before 1.0 even identifies the opportunity.
Sec_collapse and momentum_fade exits require daily monitoring — they can turn a winning trade into a loss if missed. Run the daily exit monitor every day for 3.0 positions.
Best months: August (81% WR, EV=+14.6%), March (75%, EV=+14.5%), September (80%, EV=+10.8%). January and February are weak (22–29% WR).
4.0Dark Horse+266% · $366K · 281 trades · +274% alpha
Platinum tier (score ≥16) requires PLATINUM Bonus (+5pts) which needs (Zone or Emerged) AND any Phoenix signal AND rank <12. Perhaps 3–5 per year. When Platinum appears: 2.2% risk, immediate execution, hold to full TP.
Exit cascade by EV: rs_high (100% WR, +47.6%) → rs_matured (100%, +21.3%) → timeout day 70+ with positive P&L (97.7%, +20.7%) → 2R partial (100%) → mom_fade (88.9%) → sec_collapse (42% — downgrade) → stop (0%).
January (27% WR) and December (30% WR) are danger months. Do not deploy new 4.0 in November–January without Grade 14+ score. July (85% WR) and February (78% WR) are best.
The 4.0 day-70+ timeout upgrade (→★5, 97.7% WR) is checked daily. A 4.0 trade with positive P&L at day 70 should be held — this is near-certain to close profitably.
Sec_collapse is the primary risk (71 occurrences in 4.0 alone). Run daily exit monitor without fail.
5.0Phoenix+128% · $227K · 260 trades
Detects RS collapse 35%+ from 52-week peak with first recovery signal (RS crossing its 10-day EMA for the first time in 30+ days below). Deep Phoenix (50%+ collapse) scores higher (+7pts vs +5pts) — the larger the collapse, the larger the potential re-rating.
Prior-peak recovery exit is unique to 5.0: close when RS has recovered 80%+ of the collapse distance back to the prior RS peak. Captures the full round-trip.
March (84% WR, EV=+14.8%) is 5.0's single best month. July and August also strong. December (36%) and October (42%) are weak — reduce or skip new 5.0 entries in these months.
Stop is identical to all versions: entry × 0.93. The 7% stop is not widened for deeper collapses — the collapse has already occurred. You are entering the recovery, not the collapse.

⚡ The 2R Partial Exit — Single Most Important Action

When price reaches Entry + 2 × (Entry − Stop): sell exactly 50%, move stop to entry price. This action converts the trade from "possible winner" into "guaranteed winner." The remaining 50% has zero downside.

Target execution rate: 100%. Every missed 2R partial is permanently lost guaranteed profit. After executing, update Live Grade to "→★5 (2R hit [date])". All versions: 2.0 (100% WR, 29 occurrences), 3.0 (100%, 9), 4.0 (100%, 64), 5.0 (100%, 21).
